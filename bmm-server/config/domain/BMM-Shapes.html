This document describes the OSLC ResourceShape constraints applied to the OMG Business Motivation Model (BMM) 1.3 vocabulary. These constraints govern the BMM resources managed by the bmm-server, an OSLC 3.0 server implementation built on the oslc-service Express middleware.

The shapes define, for each BMM resource type, which properties are required, which are optional, the allowed cardinality, value types, and whether each property is a link to another resource or a literal value. They also drive the server's creation dialogs, creation factories, and OSLC query capabilities.

This document is provided as user documentation for the bmm-server, an OSLC 3.0 server for the OMG Business Motivation Model. It is not an OMG or OASIS standard — it describes a specific implementation of OSLC ResourceShapes over the BMM vocabulary.

Introduction

The OMG Business Motivation Model [[BMM]] provides a scheme for developing, communicating, and managing business plans in an organized manner. It captures the relationships between an enterprise's Ends (what it wants to achieve), its Means (how it intends to achieve them), and the Influencers and Assessments that shape business motivation.

The BMM is defined as a UML model with Structured English fact types. To make it usable as a linked-data vocabulary, the bmm-server publishes an RDFS representation of the BMM vocabulary (BMM.ttl) and accompanying OSLC ResourceShapes (BMM-Shapes.ttl) that constrain how BMM resources are authored, linked, and validated.

RDF vocabularies are intentionally open: they define terms but do not specify cardinality, required vs. optional properties, or the expected types of property values. These decisions are made by OSLC ResourceShapes [[OSLCShapes]], which close down the vocabulary for a particular purpose. Different deployments can apply different sets of constraints to the same vocabulary without modifying the vocabulary itself.

All BMM resource types inherit from bmm:MotivationalElement, which in turn is a subclass of oslc_am:Resource [[OSLCAM]]. This gives every BMM resource the standard OSLC Core properties (dcterms:title, dcterms:description, dcterms:identifier, dcterms:creator, dcterms:created, dcterms:modified, and so on) in addition to its BMM-specific properties.

Terminology

Terminology for BMM resources (Vision, Goal, Objective, Mission, Strategy, Tactic, Business Policy, Business Rule, Influencer, Assessment, Potential Impact, Organization Unit, Business Process, Asset) is defined by the OMG BMM specification [[BMM]]. Terminology for OSLC resources and ResourceShape constructs is defined by the OSLC Core specification [[!OSLCCore3]] and its ResourceShape part [[!OSLCShapes]].

BMM Resource Organization

The BMM vocabulary defines four groups of motivational elements. Each resource shape in this document belongs to one of these groups:

Ends — what the enterprise wants to achieve
Vision, Goal, Objective
Means — how the enterprise intends to achieve its Ends
Mission, Strategy, Tactic, Business Policy, Business Rule
Influencers and Assessments — factors shaping the Ends and Means
Influencer, Assessment, Potential Impact
Organization, Process, and Asset — who, how, and with what
Organization Unit, Business Process, Asset

Inherited OSLC Core and Architecture Management properties (title, description, identifier, creator, contributor, created, modified, subject, type, instanceShape, serviceProvider, shortTitle) are included on every BMM shape and are not repeated in the prose descriptions below.

Resource Constraints

This section specifies the constraints for each BMM resource shape served by bmm-server. The resource properties are not limited to the ones defined here; BMM servers may provide additional properties. Any additional properties should exist in their own unique namespace and not use the namespaces defined in these specifications.

Resource: Vision

A bmm:Vision describes the future state of the enterprise, without regard to how it is to be achieved — the ultimate, possibly unattainable, state the enterprise would like to reach. A Vision is amplified by Goals and made operative by a Mission.

Resource: Goal

A bmm:Goal is a statement about a state or condition of the enterprise to be brought about or sustained through appropriate Means. A Goal amplifies a Vision and is quantified by one or more Objectives. Goals may be decomposed into sub-Goals.

Resource: Objective

A bmm:Objective is a statement of an attainable, time-targeted, and measurable target that the enterprise seeks to meet in order to achieve its Goals. An Objective quantifies a Goal and typically has a target date and a measure of progress. Objectives may be decomposed into sub-Objectives.

Resource: Mission

A bmm:Mission indicates the ongoing operational activity of the enterprise — what the business is or will be doing on a day-to-day basis. A Mission makes a Vision operative and is planned by means of Strategies.

Resource: Strategy

A bmm:Strategy represents the essential Course of Action to achieve a Goal. Strategies channel efforts toward Goals (and occasionally toward the Vision), are governed by Directives (Business Policies and Business Rules), and are realized by Tactics. Strategies may be composed of sub-Strategies.

Resource: Tactic

A bmm:Tactic is a Course of Action that represents part of the detailing of Strategies. A Tactic implements a Strategy, is governed by Directives, and may effect the enforcement of a Business Rule. Tactics may be composed of sub-Tactics.

Resource: Business Policy

A bmm:BusinessPolicy is a Directive that is not directly enforceable and whose purpose is to govern or guide the enterprise. Business Policies govern Courses of Action and Business Processes, and serve as the basis for practicable Business Rules.

Resource: Business Rule

A bmm:BusinessRule is a Directive, intended to govern, guide, or influence business behavior, in support of a Business Policy. Business Rules are discrete, atomic, and practicable; they guide Business Processes and have a specified enforcement level (strictly enforced, deferred enforcement, override with explanation, and so on).

Resource: Influencer

A bmm:Influencer is anything that has the capability to produce an effect on the enterprise's employment of Means or achievement of Ends, without direct exercise of command. Influencers are neutral; Assessments are required to judge their impact. Common categories include Competition, Customer, Environment, Partner, Regulation, Supplier, and Technology (external) and Assumption, Corporate Value, Habit, Infrastructure, Issue, Management Prerogative, and Resource (internal).

Resource: Assessment

A bmm:Assessment is a judgment of some Influencer that affects the organization's ability to employ its Means or achieve its Ends. Assessments are typically categorized using SWOT — Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, or Threat — and identify Potential Impacts, which in turn provide the impetus for Directives.

Resource: Potential Impact

A bmm:PotentialImpact quantifies or qualifies a specific aspect of an Assessment. Potential Impacts are categorized as Risks (probability of loss) or Potential Rewards (probability of gain) and can provide impetus for Directives that govern Courses of Action or support the achievement of Ends.

Resource: Organization Unit

A bmm:OrganizationUnit is any named group of people within the enterprise with a purpose and a budget. Organization Units may be responsible for Ends, establish Means, recognize Influencers, make Assessments, and be defined by Business Processes. Organization Units may themselves contain sub-Organization Units.

Resource: Business Process

A bmm:BusinessProcess realizes a Course of Action — it provides the detail of activities, sequencing, control, and synchronization that enable the enterprise to carry out its Strategies and Tactics. Business Processes are governed by Business Policies and Business Rules and may realize Assets.

Resource: Asset

A bmm:Asset is a thing of value used in operating the enterprise — either a Fixed Asset (equipment, buildings, patents, licenses) or a Resource (raw materials, parts, finished goods, cash). Assets are deployed by Courses of Action and realized by Business Processes.

Conformance

A bmm-server implementation MUST follow the constraints defined here where required, and with the meanings defined here. A bmm-server MAY provide additional constraints for specific purposes, and MAY provide additional properties beyond those defined in the shapes — these additional properties SHOULD exist in their own unique namespace and SHOULD NOT use the bmm, oslc, or oslc_am namespaces.
